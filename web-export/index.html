The Journey of a Meal

🦷 Mouth

Structures: Teeth, tongue, salivary glands.

Process: Teeth mechanically break food into smaller pieces (mastication). Salivary amylase begins chemical digestion of starches. Food mixes with saliva to form a soft ball called a bolus.

Problems: Cavities, dry mouth (xerostomia), poor chewing leading to incomplete digestion.

🪡 Esophagus

Structures: Muscular tube, lower esophageal sphincter.

Process: Peristalsis (rhythmic muscle contractions) pushes the bolus from the throat to the stomach. The sphincter prevents stomach acid from backing up.

Problems: GERD (acid reflux), difficulty swallowing (dysphagia).

🫙 Stomach

Structures: Gastric glands, pyloric sphincter.

Process: Gastric acid (HCl) and pepsin break proteins. Churning mixes everything into a semi-liquid called chyme. The pyloric sphincter releases chyme slowly into the small intestine.

Problems: Ulcers, gastritis, delayed emptying (gastroparesis).

🧪 Small Intestine & Accessory Organs

Structures: Duodenum, jejunum, ileum; liver, gallbladder, pancreas.

Process: Bile (from gallbladder) emulsifies fats. Pancreatic enzymes break down proteins, fats, and carbohydrates. Villi and microvilli absorb nutrients (amino acids, glucose, fatty acids) into the bloodstream and lymph.

Problems: Celiac disease, Crohn's disease, lactose intolerance, gallstones.

💧 Large Intestine

Structures: Cecum, colon (ascending, transverse, descending, sigmoid), appendix.

Process: Absorbs water and electrolytes from remaining indigestible material. Gut bacteria ferment fiber and produce some B vitamins and vitamin K. Remaining material forms feces.

Problems: IBS, colitis, constipation, colorectal cancer, diverticulitis.

🚽 Rectum & Anus

Structures: Rectum, internal and external anal sphincters.

Process: Feces are stored in the rectum until stretch receptors trigger the defecation reflex. The internal sphincter relaxes involuntarily; the external sphincter is under voluntary control, allowing timed elimination.

Problems: Hemorrhoids, rectal prolapse, fecal incontinence.
